Fetch Catalog Technical Challenge

Introduction

The aim of this coding challenge is to get a sense of how you approach the problems posed below. Submit your results in any way you please - .sql files, Jupyter notebooks, .txt files & PDF are all acceptable formats. (For folks submitting an Excel version of this challenge, please include a PDF showing the text for the formula that you've used to answer each question, along with the workbook with the results.)

Be prepared to walk through your responses to these questions with your interview panelists. Anything you include in these responses, along with strategies that you may not have pursued, are potential topics of discussion with your panelists.

This challenge is intended to take less than 2 hours to complete, but feel free to spend as much time as you’d like on the responses below. There is no time limit on completing this challenge.

Data

Included with this challenge are four CSV files that you can upload to your preferred database engine. All engines and corresponding SQL dialects are accepted, just let us know what you choose.

Questions

General questions

  1. What are your general observations about the quality & completeness of the data included in this challenge?
  2. Please develop 1-2 metrics that grade the quality of data presented in one of these tables. The `products` or `brands` tables are a good candidate for this.

Products-related questions

The following few questions can be solved by looking solely at the products table.

  1. What are the most popular category-1, -2 & -3 combinations from the `products` table? Does that ranking change if you only include the top 2 levels of categories? What if you only include the top level?
  2. How many products appear to have a mis-match between the size as listed in the size field and the text in the product field?
  3. For products with a null size value, how many products’ size can you extract from its name? Select these records in such a way that shows this size in a new column & removes it from the `product_name` field.
  4. How many products appear to have a mis-match between its barcode and a UPC-like identifier in its name?

Questions spanning more than one table

You’re expected to join across a few tables to answer the following questions.

  1. How many products are associated with a recognized CPG partner? What are your observations about these partner products?
  2. How many products are associated with a record in the brands table? What are the 3 most popular categories from this set, as defined by the category from the `brands` table?

Optional questions

The following more advanced questions are completely optional for this challenge. More advanced applicants are welcome to show off their SQL-chops in these responses.

  1. How many products are associated with a `related brand` from the brands table?
  2. The logging “timestamps” on the `brands` table are stored as an epoch timestamp. Create new columns to store these values in a format that better aligns with the rest of this schema.
